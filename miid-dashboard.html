Overview

Client was quite uncomfortable with checking data by firing already made queries as few people just don't like doing technical stuff. So they required to prepare a Dashboard where they just have to Login on to the tool and refresh the Dashboard. They required things like net sales value, net sales unit, sales through different channels and the trends for last week and last year.

Taking into considerations all the various Visualization Design principles we had to make the Dashboard more user friendly by providing Visualizations which were smooth and easy to navigate. I was working under my senior colleagues who were both Data Visualizers and Designers. Following were some of the Visualization principles which we took into consideration while designing this Dashboard:

1. Understand the context (ask good questions!)
2. Choose an appropriate visual display (consider the purpose)
3. Remove the clutter (borders, gridlines, data markers, axis labels, color)
4. Focus attention where you want it (where is attention drawn?)
5. Think like a designer (data visualization = science + art)
6. Tell a story (context, nuances, meaning)

Following these basic principles helped us to tell and show the data in a meaningful way suitable for the targeted audience to understand.
