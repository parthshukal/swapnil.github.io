UX Studio Design Challenge

In May 2018, I had applied for UX Designer position at UX Studio Team. After applying via their career site, I got a reply from them within a week and they provided me with a Design challenge.
‍
My challenge was to design screens for a To-Do application. I had the freedom of choosing the device for the design. I went for iOS devices. They had provided me with the User Insights and the basic Wireframes. I just had to design the screens based on the wireframes they had provided.

Overview

This was first time I was into a design challenge. It was pretty exciting and I got to use Sketch this time for designing the app screens. This challenge, I thought, was more of User Interface(UI) oriented than User Experience(UX) as the User Insights and Wireframes were already provided and I didn't had to much of a research. It was mainly about converting those Wireframes into app screens and bring them to life.

Although, the Product Design team at UX Studio Team liked my designs, and an interview was setup for the same, I didn't made the final cut. It was a great experience though, as it was my first interview for an UX Designer position and took some real good positives back with me.

Design Process

User Insights

As mentioned earlier, the Client already provided me with the User Insights, which are listed below:

1. Usually I forget what things I have to do day-by-day. I write them down on a piece of paper, but I always lose them.
‍
2. I use apps to know what I have to do that day, week or month. However sometimes I find it hard to use those apps, as I want to add or delete todo-s very quickly. It’s not that simple when I have to click or tap a lot.
‍
3. I want to differentiate somehow the importance of each todo-s. I can’t do it in my head.
‍
4. I don’t use anything to organize my things to do. Of course, sometimes I forget some things, but I don’t have time to write and organize little notes in an app, it would take too much of my precious time. I guess.
‍
5. I’d really need something that tells me what things I have to do and what are those things which are even more important than the others. It stresses me to keep them in my mind.

User Flows

Based on the User Insights and Requirements I was able to create task flows. Task flows are very important in understanding:

1. Your users’ goals and what they are trying to achieve

2. The steps that your users currently take in order to achieve their goals (very helpful to see how they follow instructions or have devised ways to work around problems in current practices!)

3. The personal, social and cultural experiences that users bring to the tasks

4. The influence of the physical environment on the users while attempting to meet a goal
